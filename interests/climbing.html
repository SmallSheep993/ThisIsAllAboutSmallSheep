Rock Climbing

Approaching vertical challenges with an analytical mind and physical precision.

Vertical Problem Solving

In the climbing community, we don't just call routes "paths"—we call them "problems." Whether I am bouldering or top-roping, climbing is a full-body engagement that requires high levels of focus, spatial awareness, and strategic planning. It is the perfect counterweight to my academic studies at Rutgers, demanding the same logical breakdown of a challenge before execution.

My Climbing Focus

Bouldering

Power • Technique • Short Routes

I enjoy the intensity of bouldering, where every move counts. It's about finding the most efficient "beta" (solution) to navigate a difficult sequence of holds.

Top Roping

Endurance • Height • Safety

For longer routes, I focus on steady breathing and efficient movement to manage energy levels over 40–50 foot climbs.

The "Beta" Mindset

Analysis • Execution • Iteration

Just like code, if a move doesn't work, I "debug" my body position. I adjust my center of gravity or foot placement until the problem is solved.
