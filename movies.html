My Favorites

The Godfather (1972)

Director: Francis Ford Coppola

An epic masterpiece of the Corleone family. It's not just a crime film; it's a profound story about family, loyalty, and the nature of power.

The Godfather Part II (1974)

Director: Francis Ford Coppola

A rare sequel that stands as high as the original, charting the parallel lives of Vito and Michael Corleone across different eras.

The Godfather Part III (1990)

Director: Francis Ford Coppola

The final chapter of Michael Corleone's journey as he seeks to legitimize his empire and find redemption for his past sins.

Titanic (1997)

Director: James Cameron

A grand, tragic romance set against the ill-fated maiden voyage of the R.M.S. Titanic. A spectacle of cinema and emotion.

Léon: The Professional (1994)

Director: Luc Besson

An unlikely bond between a professional assassin and a young girl. A powerful, stylistic story of protection and sacrifice.

The Shawshank Redemption (1994)

Director: Frank Darabont

A profound story of hope and friendship that transcends the walls of a prison. "Fear can hold you prisoner, hope can set you free."
